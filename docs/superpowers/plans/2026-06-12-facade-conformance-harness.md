# agents.x-k8s.io Facade: Conformance Harness (issue #19, PR13, W2 slice 2)

> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended) or superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax for tracking.

**Goal:** The acceptance core of #19: prove an upstream agent-sandbox user can apply their manifests UNCHANGED against our facade and get identical observable behavior, with every divergence documented and none silent. This slice vendors the upstream conformance artifacts (their `examples/` + `test/e2e/`), adds a `facade-conformance` CI job that deploys our facade + the `agents.x-k8s.io` CRDs on kind and applies their example Sandbox manifests unchanged, asserting our facade accepts and reconciles them through the object-level lifecycle (admission, the husk-backed claim created, replicas 0/1, status conditions, deletion), and writes the conformance matrix in `docs/facade-conformance.md`. The in-VM tail of their suite (their predicates that wait for `PodReady`/`ChromeReady`, i.e. the sandbox actually running) hits the same nested-VMM boundary as #18 on kind and is documented as needing a KVM-capable kubelet (bare-metal reference node), not silently skipped. Verified: their example manifests apply unchanged and our facade reconciles them to the kind-achievable object state; the conformance matrix records every upstream test/example as proven-object-level-on-kind, needs-bare-metal, or a justified exception.

**Honesty constraint:** "never silent divergence" is the rule. Every upstream conformance test and example is enumerated in `docs/facade-conformance.md` with an explicit status: PROVEN object-level on kind (their manifest applied unchanged, our facade reconciles it, the lifecycle/replicas/status/deletion match), NEEDS bare-metal (their predicate waits for the pod/sandbox to actually run, which needs a KVM-capable kubelet, the documented #18 nested-VMM boundary), or a JUSTIFIED EXCEPTION (a field or behavior we map differently, with the reason). We do NOT claim full conformance; we claim object-level conformance on kind + a documented bare-metal path for the in-VM tail, with the matrix as the no-silent-divergence ledger. We pin their latest two minors (per the issue) and state the pinned versions. The "identical observable behavior" claim is scoped to what each test actually asserts: where their test asserts an object-level fact, we prove it; where it asserts a running-sandbox fact, we document the bare-metal requirement. No number or pass-claim that a CI job does not demonstrate.

**Architecture:** Vendor `third_party/agent-sandbox/` already holds their CRDs + a couple examples; extend it with their full `examples/` (the apply-unchanged manifests) + their `test/e2e/` (the conformance reference, including the framework). A `facade-conformance` kind CI job: build + load our facade image, deploy the facade + the `agents.x-k8s.io` CRDs (from third_party) + our stack, `kubectl apply -f` their example Sandbox manifests UNCHANGED, and assert object-level: the Sandbox is admitted, our facade creates the bridged husk-backed SandboxClaim, the Sandbox status mirrors the run-path state achievable on kind, replicas 0 terminates, deletion GCs. Optionally run their `test/e2e` Go framework against our facade for the object-level-tractable subset (if their framework can target our cluster via kubeconfig without their controller); where their predicates require `PodReady`, document the bare-metal requirement. `docs/facade-conformance.md` holds the per-test/per-example matrix.

**Context for the implementer:**
- Upstream: `sigs.k8s.io/agent-sandbox@v0.4.6` in the module cache (`$(go env GOMODCACHE)/sigs.k8s.io/agent-sandbox@v0.4.6/`). `test/e2e/{replicas,shutdown,parallelism,volumeclaimtemplate,chromesandbox}_test.go` use `framework.NewTestContext(t)` (a kubeconfig-targeting framework in `test/e2e/framework`). `examples/` (hello-world.yaml, the extensions/examples, etc.) are the apply-unchanged manifests. The Sandbox is `agents.x-k8s.io/v1alpha1` (podTemplate + replicas 0/1 + lifecycle + service). The CRDs are in their `config/crd` or vendored in `third_party/agent-sandbox/crds`.
- Our facade (#76, slice 1): `internal/facade` (SandboxReconciler maps Sandbox -> our husk-backed SandboxClaim, mirrors status, the mitos.run/pool bridge), `cmd/facade` (the opt-in binary), `deploy/facade/` (the Deployment), `third_party/agent-sandbox/` (vendored CRDs + examples), `docs/facade-conformance.md` (the approach, written in slice 1; this slice fills the matrix). `docs/adr/0001-facade-and-naming.md`.
- The #18 nested-VMM boundary: kind nodes are containers; a husk pod's Firecracker cannot boot nested, so a husk-backed Sandbox does not reach in-VM Ready on kind (the object lifecycle works; the running-sandbox tail needs bare metal). Mirror the honest split the husk slices used (kind-e2e-husk gates the object lifecycle; KVM/bare-metal for in-VM).
