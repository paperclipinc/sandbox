**Goal:** Complete the W2 facade (#19): map the upstream agent-sandbox EXTENSION kinds onto our engine so a user's upstream SandboxTemplate, SandboxWarmPool, and SandboxClaim manifests are fulfilled by our pool + fork-from-snapshot run path, with behavioral fidelity (status conditions, identity, storage contract, deletion) matched. The facade already maps their core Sandbox (slices 1-3); this slice adds the extension reconcilers: their SandboxTemplate (extensions.agents.x-k8s.io) to our SandboxTemplate, their SandboxWarmPool to our SandboxPool (replicas + templateRef), and their SandboxClaim to our SandboxClaim (claim a sandbox from a warm pool per the warmpool policy none/default/named). Verified in envtest + the facade-conformance kind job extended with their extension example manifests applied unchanged: a SandboxTemplate creates our template, a SandboxWarmPool maintains our pool at the requested replicas, and a SandboxClaim binds a sandbox from the pool, with status conditions + deletion mirrored at the object level. This closes #19's facade-controller + behavioral-fidelity tasks; the in-VM running-sandbox tail stays the documented bare-metal path (the #18 boundary), recorded in the conformance matrix.

**Honesty constraint:** the extension mappings are proven at the OBJECT level on kind (their extension objects reconcile to our pool/claim/template objects; the lifecycle, status conditions, and deletion match the conformant observable), with the running-sandbox tail (a claim actually serving a booted VM) on the bare-metal reference node (#18 nested-VMM boundary), recorded in docs/facade-conformance.md with no silent divergence. Where our pool/claim semantics differ from theirs (e.g. our fork-from-snapshot vs their pod template, our pool fields vs their warmpool policy), the difference is a documented justified exception in the matrix, not a silent remap. The warmpool policy (none/default/named) is honored as their API specifies (none => fresh, default => any matching pool, named => that pool); if our engine cannot honor a policy exactly, state it. Every conformance claim maps to what the facade-conformance job asserts; the bench/facade latency advantage (slice 3) is unchanged. No upstream field is silently dropped; unmapped fields are listed.

**Architecture:** Three new controller-runtime reconcilers in `internal/facade`, each `For(&<their extension kind>{})` and creating/owning our corresponding `mitos.run` object, mirroring status + handling deletion via owner refs:
- SandboxTemplateReconciler: their SandboxTemplate (extensions.agents.x-k8s.io/v1alpha1) -> our v1alpha1.SandboxTemplate (the podTemplate/image/spec mapped onto our template fields; unmapped fields documented).
- SandboxWarmPoolReconciler: their SandboxWarmPool (replicas + sandboxTemplateRef + updateStrategy) -> our SandboxPool (Replicas + the template ref resolved to our template; the bridge annotation mitos.run/pool); HPA-controlled replicas are honored (we read their spec.replicas).
- SandboxClaimReconciler (facade): their SandboxClaim (sandboxTemplateRef + lifecycle + warmpool policy + additionalPodMetadata) -> our SandboxClaim (claim from the pool/template; the warmpool policy selects the pool: none => a fresh non-pooled claim, default => any pool for the template, named => that pool); mirror their status (the bound Sandbox/serviceFQDN/conditions) + deletion.
The facade binary (cmd/facade) registers all reconcilers; they are opt-in. The conformance job applies their extension examples unchanged.
